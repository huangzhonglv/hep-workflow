developments in BSM phenomenology and neutrino physics. This file helps
the idea generator suggest timely, concrete topics.

Consult this file when the user's direction is vague or when you need to
suggest specific sub-topics within a broad area.

## 1. BSM Models with New Gauge Bosons

### Active Z' model classes
- **U(1)_{B-L}**: Well-studied but still has unexplored corners, especially
  when combined with non-minimal neutrino sectors or specific scalar breaking
  patterns
- **U(1)_{L_μ - L_τ}**: Motivated by muon g-2; interplay with neutrino
  oscillation data (NOvA, T2K, JUNO, DUNE) is an active area
- **U(1)_X (generalized charges)**: Family-dependent U(1) extensions with
  anomaly cancellation conditions constraining the charge assignments;
  systematic phenomenological surveys of allowed charge patterns
- **Sequential Standard Model Z'**: LHC direct search reinterpretations
  with updated Run 3 data
- **Dark Z' / dark photon**: Models where Z' mediates interactions with a
  dark sector; connections to dark matter and beam dump experiments

### Open questions and opportunities
- Combined analysis of LHC dilepton + dijet + ditau searches in a single
  model framework (often done channel by channel)
- Z' models with non-universal couplings: mapping the full parameter space
  against LHC + flavor + EW precision simultaneously
- One-loop corrections to Z' production and decay in specific models
  (often only tree-level results exist)
- Z'-induced effects in neutrino scattering experiments (COHERENT, etc.)

### Recent experimental inputs
- LHC Run 3 high-mass dilepton and dijet resonance searches
- COHERENT CEvNS measurements constraining light mediators
- Updated muon g-2 combination

## 2. Extended Scalar Sectors

### Active model classes
- **Two-Higgs-Doublet Models (2HDM)**: Types I-IV plus aligned and
  flavor-specific variants; constraints from Higgs coupling measurements,
  flavor physics, and direct heavy Higgs searches
- **Inert Doublet Model (IDM)**: Provides dark matter candidate; interplay
  with direct detection, collider, and relic density constraints
- **Higgs triplet models**: Scalar triplets generating neutrino mass
  (Type-II seesaw); same-sign dilepton signatures at LHC
- **Georgi-Machacek model**: Custodial-symmetry-preserving triplets;
  distinct collider phenomenology
- **Singlet extensions**: Real/complex singlet + SM; implications for
  EWPT and gravitational wave signals

### Open questions and opportunities
- Updated global fits of 2HDM parameter space with full LHC Run 2 + Run 3
  Higgs data
- Interplay between Higgs precision measurements and flavor constraints
  in specific 2HDM types
- Di-Higgs production in extended scalar sectors at HL-LHC sensitivity
- Vacuum stability analysis in multi-scalar models with radiative neutrino
  mass generation
- CP violation in extended scalar sectors: EDM constraints vs collider
  signatures

## 3. Leptoquarks

### Active model classes
- **Scalar leptoquarks**: S_1, S_3, R_2, \tilde{R}_2 — classified by SM
  quantum numbers; different flavor anomaly explanations
- **Vector leptoquarks**: U_1, U_3 — arise naturally in Pati-Salam and
  other GUT frameworks
- **Combined leptoquark scenarios**: Multiple leptoquarks to simultaneously
  address several anomalies

### Open questions and opportunities
- Leptoquark contributions to neutrino mass at loop level (1-loop and
  2-loop radiative mass generation)
- Leptoquark pair production at LHC Run 3: updated mass limits and
  reinterpretation in specific flavor textures
- Single leptoquark production channels: sensitivity to coupling structure
- Leptoquark effects on high-p_T tails in pp → ℓℓ and pp → ℓν
- Leptoquark models satisfying proton decay constraints in GUT embeddings

## 4. Neutrino Mass Mechanisms

### Seesaw variants
- **Type-I seesaw**: Heavy right-handed neutrinos; low-scale variants
  with testable signatures (displaced vertices, lepton number violation)
- **Type-II seesaw**: Scalar triplet; same-sign dilepton signature at
  colliders; connection to leptogenesis
- **Type-III seesaw**: Fermion triplet; multilepton signatures at LHC
- **Inverse seesaw**: Naturally small neutrino masses with TeV-scale
  particles; enhanced lepton flavor violation
- **Linear seesaw**: Similar to inverse seesaw with different parameter
  structure; less explored phenomenologically

### Radiative mass generation
- **Scotogenic model (Ma, 2006)**: Neutrino mass at one loop with inert
  doublet + right-handed neutrinos; dark matter connection
- **Two-loop models**: Zee model, Zee-Babu model, cocktail model;
  richer scalar sectors with distinctive collider signatures
- **Three-loop models**: Krauss-Nasri-Trodden type; highly predictive
  due to many new particles required
- **Systematic classification**: Identifying new topologies for radiative
  mass generation that haven't been explored

### Open questions and opportunities
- Detailed collider phenomenology of low-scale seesaw models with
  updated LHC data
- Interplay between neutrino mass generation mechanism and leptogenesis
  in specific models
- Radiative neutrino mass models with dark matter candidates:
  comprehensive parameter space scans
- Lepton flavor violation (μ → eγ, μ → 3e, μ-e conversion) predictions
  in specific mass generation mechanisms, confronted with MEG II, Mu3e,
  COMET/Mu2e sensitivity
- Neutrino mass models embedded in larger gauge frameworks (B-L, L_μ-L_τ)

## 5. Neutrino Oscillation Phenomenology

### Current experimental landscape
- **Atmospheric**: Super-Kamiokande, IceCube DeepCore
- **Accelerator**: NOvA, T2K (completed/completing data-taking)
- **Reactor**: Daya Bay (completed), JUNO (commissioning/running)
- **Next generation**: DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, ESSnuSB

### Key open questions
- **Mass ordering**: Normal vs inverted — current hints favor normal
  from NOvA+T2K+reactor combination; JUNO and DUNE will resolve
- **CP violation phase δ_CP**: Current status from T2K and NOvA;
  tension between datasets; DUNE sensitivity projections
- **θ_{23} octant**: Above or below 45°; implications for flavor
  symmetries
- **Unitarity of PMNS matrix**: Non-unitarity from heavy neutral
  leptons; testable with precision oscillation data
- **NSI (Non-Standard Interactions)**: New physics effects in
  neutrino propagation; constraints from oscillation + scattering data

### Open questions and opportunities
- Global fit of oscillation parameters including latest NOvA/T2K data
  in the presence of specific BSM scenarios (NSI, sterile neutrinos)
- Sensitivity projections for DUNE/HK to specific new physics models
  (not just generic NSI parameters, but derived from concrete UV models)
- Combined analysis of oscillation + COHERENT + LHC data to constrain
  mediator models affecting neutrino interactions
- Non-standard neutrino interactions from specific Z' or leptoquark
  models: computing the NSI parameters from fundamental couplings and
  mapping onto oscillation observables

## 6. Connections: Neutrino Mass × BSM Models

This is the intersection that often produces the most interesting and
novel research — linking a specific BSM model to the neutrino sector.

### High-potential directions
- **Z' models with radiative neutrino mass**: The Z' controls the
  symmetry breaking that generates neutrino mass at loop level.
  Many specific constructions remain unstudied.
- **Scotogenic + gauge extensions**: Adding a U(1)_{B-L} or
  U(1)_{L_μ-L_τ} to the scotogenic model; the Z' mass and coupling
  are related to the neutrino mass scale
- **Leptoquark-induced neutrino mass**: Specific leptoquark
  representations that contribute to neutrino mass at 1 or 2 loops;
  connecting flavor anomaly explanations with neutrino mass
- **2HDM + seesaw**: Two-Higgs-doublet model providing the scalar
  sector for a seesaw mechanism; interplay between Higgs phenomenology
  and neutrino mass
- **Dark matter + neutrino mass**: Models where the dark matter
  candidate is part of the neutrino mass generation mechanism
  (scotogenic paradigm and its extensions)

### Concrete project seeds
These are specific enough to become papers:

1. "Constraints on U(1)_{L_μ-L_τ} scotogenic model from JUNO + muon g-2
   + LHC dark photon searches" — combine three independent probes in a
   single model framework

2. "Two-loop neutrino mass from scalar leptoquark S_1: predictions for
   MEG II and Mu2e" — compute the neutrino mass matrix and LFV rates,
   derive correlations

3. "Heavy neutral lepton production via Z' decay at LHC: displaced vertex
   signatures in U(1)_{B-L} seesaw models" — collider study with concrete
   signal/background analysis

4. "Non-standard neutrino interactions in DUNE from a light Z' in
   U(1)_{B-3L_τ}: resolving the θ_{23} octant" — connect a UV-complete
   model to oscillation phenomenology

5. "Inert doublet model with Type-II seesaw: correlated predictions for
   dark matter direct detection and neutrinoless double beta decay" —
   two independent observables from the same model parameters

## 7. Methodology Gaps Worth Filling

These are "meta-opportunities" — known calculations or analyses that
should exist but haven't been done (or are outdated).

- NLO QCD corrections to leptoquark pair production in specific flavor
  scenarios (not just mass-degenerate simplified models)
- Electroweak precision constraints (oblique parameters) for general
  multi-scalar models: automated computation framework
- Complete one-loop matching of popular UV models onto SMEFT: many models
  still only have tree-level matching
- Updated global fits combining LHC Higgs data + EW precision + flavor
  in specific BSM scenarios (many fits use outdated data)
- Renormalization group analysis of neutrino mass model parameters:
  running effects on mixing angles and mass ordering discrimination
- Machine learning for BSM parameter space exploration: applying modern
  sampling techniques to high-dimensional scans
